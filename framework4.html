Part 1
Before You Build,
Answer These.

Speed is no longer the constraint — context is.

In an AI-native environment, teams can build almost anything. The real challenge is defining the right problem, at the right time, with clear success criteria — before a single sprint begins.

These three anchors ensure every decision starts with shared context — not assumptions.

Anchor 01
What problem are we actually solving?
Anchor 02
Why this problem — for this company, right now?
Anchor 03
What does success look like — and for whom?
Part 2
The Four-Stage
Operating System

Each stage produces structured context — not static documents — with a clear question, an AI role, and a decision gate. You don't move forward until the context is complete.

Stage 01
Explore
Is this worth solving?
"Do we have enough signal — across users, business, and market — to justify solving this now?"
Context
Signal Brief — problem definition and signal synthesis
AI Role
Synthesize signals across users, market, and data. Surface patterns, contradictions, and gaps in understanding.
Gate to Pass
All three anchors answered with evidence. Go/no-go decided.
Stage 02
Validate
Does our solution actually work?
"What are the highest-risk assumptions — and what is the fastest way to validate or invalidate them?"
Context
Prototype Brief + success bar
AI Role
Generate experiment options, define success criteria, and explore solution variations.
Gate to Pass
Success criteria are defined before building — not after results are observed.
Stage 03
Build
How do we ship it right?
"What are we saying no to — and does the whole team understand the tradeoffs?"
Context
Release Narrative — living doc
AI Role
Generate spec draft, surface edge cases, identify tradeoffs, flag risks.
Gate to Pass
Tradeoffs explicit and understood by every builder on the team.
Stage 04
Iterate
What you ship is never the end: Learn → Iterate
Every signal from production feeds the next decision — what to double down on, what to kill, what to explore next.
Context
Signal Log — living doc
AI Role
Read production signals, surface behavioral patterns, flag anchor drift.
Gate to Pass
Learnings fed back into next Explore cycle. Nothing ships into a void.
Part 3
Everyone Builds.
Not Everyone Decides.

In AI-native teams, execution is shared — across engineers, designers, and AI. Judgment is not.

This system defines where decisions are made, what context they rely on, and who is accountable at each stage — so speed increases without losing clarity.

✦ For AI Products Only
AI Product
Add-On Layer
Building with AI isn't just a technical decision — it's a quality commitment. This layer defines the standards for accuracy, reliability, and output quality — before anything ships.
Evaluation plan
What does "good" look like for this AI feature? Define accuracy benchmarks, success metrics, and what failure modes you're willing to accept before you build.
→ Stage 2 gate
Guardrails design
When the AI is wrong — and it will be — what happens? Who decides? What's the fallback? Non-deterministic systems need explicit failure design, not just happy-path specs.
→ Stage 3 gate
Model decision log
Why this model, not that one? What capability did you trade for cost or latency? Document the reasoning so future teams understand the constraints you were working within.
→ Stage 2 + 3
Startup Evaluation plan only. Define what "good" looks like before you ship.
Growth Evaluation plan + Guardrails. Reliability matters more as user base grows.
Enterprise Full add-on activated. Model decisions need documentation for audit and handoff.
PM's irreplaceable value
What AI Can't Replace
Across Any Stage
Business constraint navigation
Understanding what the company can actually win — given team capacity, competitive position, and strategic timing. No model knows your organization's real constraints.
Problem sizing and prioritization
Answering "is this worth the bet?" relative to everything else the team could be doing. Requires judgment about opportunity cost that AI can model but not make.
Cross-functional judgment
The tradeoff decisions that span user value, technical feasibility, business viability, and team morale simultaneously. These calls belong to a human holding all four contexts at once.
